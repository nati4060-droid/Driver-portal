Module 1 — Savings in ops center hours

The ops center burns through daily calls coordinating taxis. Each call averages ~3 minutes, with added context-switch overhead between calls. The Portal automates the flow: driver requests, vehicle gets assigned, ops only approves exceptions.

Daily taxi-coordination calls~60Based on operational estimate, requires validation

Module 2 — Savings in driver time

A driver checking 4 communication channels every morning (email, WhatsApp, SMS, paper) loses time that doesn't translate to value. The Portal consolidates everything onto a single screen.

ParameterValueAssumption
Active drivers~400Order of magnitude for a national-scale operation
Daily time spent checking channels~8-12 minConservative; schedule-change days are higher
Time saved with a unified channel~75%15% remain — initial scan and read-receipt
Avg. working days per driver per year~220After vacation, holidays, weekends
Total time saved across all drivers~4,950 hours= 400 × 10 × 0.75 × 220 ÷ 60

Value of driver time: Not included in the ₪ calculation. Drivers are paid per hour, not by productivity. But that time goes back into their quality of life — a real value that doesn't need a shekel sign to be worth stating.

What the model excludes

Cost side

Development, licensing, cloud infrastructure, ongoing maintenance, training — not computed. The model is meant to estimate the upside, not net ROI. Project cost will be compared only when there is a real quote.

Revenue side

Internal improvement doesn't generate direct revenue. Reducing operational errors (missed shifts, lateness) is worth money, but wasn't included because quantifying it accurately exceeds the scope of this format.

How to read these numbers: Even in a conservative scenario that cuts assumptions by 20%, ROI stays meaningfully positive. This isn't a precise forecast — it's a sanity check, a framework for a decision, not a guarantee of outcome. Validation against real operational data is required before go-live.
